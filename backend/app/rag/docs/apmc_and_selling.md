# Selling Crops Through APMC Markets — Farmer Guide

## What is an APMC?
APMC (Agricultural Produce Market Committee) is a government-regulated wholesale market
(mandi) where farmers sell produce to licensed traders through open auction. Karnataka has
APMC yards in every district. eNAM (electronic National Agriculture Market) links many
APMCs into one online trading platform.

## Steps to sell at an APMC mandi
1. Bring produce to the APMC yard, ideally early morning when buyers are active.
2. Produce is weighed and graded (FAQ is the standard grade).
3. Traders bid in open auction; the highest bid wins.
4. You receive a sale slip; payment should be made the same day or within the time limit.
5. APMC charges a small market fee/cess; commission agents may take a percentage.

## Getting a better price
- Check live modal prices for your crop across nearby mandis before travelling.
- Compare the price at different APMCs — prices vary by demand and arrivals.
- Higher arrivals (supply) usually push prices down; sell when arrivals are lower.
- Clean, dry, well-graded produce fetches higher bids.
- Consider eNAM markets for wider buyer access and transparent online bidding.

## Karnataka eNAM and Rythu (farmer) facilities
Many Karnataka APMCs are integrated with eNAM. Farmers can get assaying (quality testing),
online payment, and access to buyers across India. Ask the APMC office for eNAM registration.

## Joint / community selling
Small farmers can pool produce through Farmer Producer Organisations (FPOs) or joint
community groups to sell in bulk, reduce transport cost per unit, and negotiate better rates.
