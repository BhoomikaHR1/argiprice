# Minimum Support Price (MSP) — Farmer Guide

## What is MSP?
MSP (Minimum Support Price) is the guaranteed price at which the Government of India
buys certain crops from farmers, regardless of market rates. It protects farmers from
distress sales when market prices crash. MSP is announced by the CCEA (Cabinet Committee
on Economic Affairs) on recommendation of the CACP (Commission for Agricultural Costs
and Prices), usually before each Kharif and Rabi season.

## Crops covered by MSP
MSP is announced for 22 mandated crops, including:
- Cereals: Paddy, Wheat, Jowar, Bajra, Maize, Ragi, Barley
- Pulses: Tur (Arhar), Moong, Urad, Gram (Chana), Masur (Lentil)
- Oilseeds: Groundnut, Soybean, Sunflower, Sesamum, Mustard, Safflower, Nigerseed
- Commercial: Cotton, Copra, Jute, Sugarcane (FRP)

## How MSP is calculated
MSP is based on the cost of production plus a margin. The government uses the A2+FL
formula (actual paid-out cost plus imputed family labour) and targets at least 1.5 times
this cost as per the 2018 policy.

## How to sell at MSP in Karnataka
1. Register on the Karnataka procurement portal or at your nearest APMC.
2. Bring land records (RTC/Pahani), Aadhaar, and a bank account linked to Aadhaar.
3. Sell during the official procurement window announced for the season.
4. Quality must meet FAQ (Fair Average Quality) norms.
5. Payment is credited directly to the registered bank account.

## Market price vs MSP
The modal price is the most commonly traded price at a mandi on a given day. If the modal
price is below MSP for an MSP crop, prefer government procurement. If market price is above
MSP, selling in the open APMC market usually earns more.
