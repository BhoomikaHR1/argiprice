# Weather, Seasons & Crop Planning — Karnataka

## Cropping seasons
- Kharif (monsoon): sown June–July, harvested October–November. Crops: paddy, maize,
  cotton, tur, groundnut, ragi, soybean.
- Rabi (winter): sown October–November, harvested February–March. Crops: wheat, gram,
  jowar, sunflower, safflower.
- Zaid (summer): short season between March and June for vegetables and fodder where
  irrigation is available.

## Using weather for decisions
- Sow with the onset of reliable rainfall; avoid sowing just before a forecast dry spell.
- Hold harvesting if heavy rain is forecast, to avoid grain damage and quality loss.
- Plan spraying on calm, dry days; rain within a few hours washes off chemicals.
- During heatwaves, irrigate in early morning or evening to reduce water loss.

## Karnataka agro-climatic notes
Karnataka has 10 agro-climatic zones, from the high-rainfall Malnad/coastal belt to the
dry northern plains. Rain-fed districts depend heavily on the southwest monsoon (June–Sept).
Choose drought-tolerant crops like ragi, jowar, and pulses in low-rainfall taluks.

## Protecting against weather risk
- Enrol in PMFBY crop insurance before the season cut-off.
- Use farm ponds and drip irrigation (Krishi Bhagya support) to buffer dry spells.
- Diversify crops so a single weather event does not wipe out all income.
